Speaker Series
Machine Learning is everywhere, from industry to academia, Computer Science to Biology. Come here about the latest findings in the field as professors and PhDs talk about their latest findings. Tutorials on the latest data science and machine learning packages! Check the calendar for when the latest talks around happening.
Tutorial
Each week, MSAIL holds seminars targeted at students lacking any formal training in Machine Learning or those wishing for a quick refresher on basic topics. These seminars are led by Nand Dhal and Santosh Mohan, and cover basic topics relating to the application of machine learning to data science problems. No theoretical familiarity with machine learning is required. Always wondered what a support vector machine is? Want to know how to predict the chances of surviving the Titanic? This is the place for you. Check the calendar for the seminar schedule.

Project Team
Signed up for EECS 442, 445, 545, or another class requiring a term paper? Think of us as your research group. Collaborate with fellow members of the MSAIL community on these papers in a forum that allow you to collaborate and connect more socially with peers in the classes you're already in! If there is enough interest, we may have a larger, year-long project that includes 10-15 people!
Dates/Times: Tuesdays, 8pm-9pm, Location TBD
Led By
Santosh Mohan
Data Science Team
Work on Kaggle competitions in the first competitive university team in the country. Advised directly by Professor Jacob Abernethy, participate in Machine Learning competitions that challenge the limits of data science. You will read papers to learn cutting-edge ML techniques used to solve problems in domains outside of Computer Science, while collaborating with others doing the same and learning a lot along the way! Visit Michigan Data Science Team (MDST) at umdst.com for more information. You can find meeting times either there, or on our schedule page.
Dates/Times: TBA
Led By
Jon C. Stroud
Reading Group
Read and discuss machine learning papers with other students from all across campus! We aim to help students study topics in machine learning beyond the scope of an intro course. Our readings will usually be focused on papers that are either fundamental in some way to the field of machine learning (the ones you see cited in textbooks!) or that explore the deeper connections between seemingly disparate subfields. Together, we'll all work hard to learn new material, drawing examples and applications from the experiences of our members.

For Fall 2015, we plan to meet Thursdays at 7pm on North Campus. Exact meeting times, locations, and papers to read can be found on the schedule page. Currently, the best way to stay informed about the reading group is to join our Facebook Group.
